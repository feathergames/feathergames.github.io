    Gastroenterology

    IBD - UC

    ScenarioManagement/Further info
    Features and associations more strongly associated with UC than Crohn'sNo inflammation beyond submucosa. Continuous disease. Crypt abscesses. PSC. Colorectal cancer. Anterior uveitis.
    Classification of flaresMild/Moderate - 4-6 stools per day, varying blood. Severe - more than 6 stools and features of systemic upset - pyrexia, tachycardia, anaemia, inflammatory markers
    Inducing remission during flare (mild/moderate) - left sided, proctosigmoiditis, proctitisTopical aminosalicylate, after 4 weeks try to add oral aminosalicylate, after 4 weeks try oral corticosteroids
    Inducing remission during flare (mild/moderate)- extensive/pancolitisTopical aminosalicylate and high dose oral aminosalicylate, after 4 weeks try stop topical and add corticosteroids
    Inducing remission during flare (severe)Should be treated in hospital. IV steroids and/or IV ciclosporin. Surgery can be considered.
    Maintaining remission (following mild/moderate, proctitis and proctosigmoiditis)Topical aminosalicylate with/without oral aminosalicylate
    Maintaining remission (following mild/moderate, left sided/extensive/pancolitis)oral aminosalicylate
    Maintaining remission (following severe or more than 2 exacerbations in a year)oral azathioprine or mercaptopurine

    IBD - Crohn's

    ScenarioManagement/Further info
    Features and associations more strongly associated with Crohn's than UCMouth to anus. Skip lesions. Inflammation of all layers. Goblet cells. Granulomas. Bowel obstruction. Fistulae/perianal disease. Episcleritis.
    Inducing remissionGlucocorticoids - topical, oral, IV. Elemental diet may be used. Aminosalicylate, azathioprine, mercaptapurine, methotrexate additional possible agents. TPMT activity assessed before mercaptapurine and azathioprine. Infliximab useful in refractory/fistulating.
    Maintaining remissionStop smoking. Azathioprine or mercaptopurine.
    Perianal diseaseAbscesses require I and D. MRI to evaluate complex fistulae. Draining seton can be used.

    Antimicrobial Side-Effects

    AntibioticSide Effects/Further info
    Macrolides (-omycin)Prolongs QT. GI side effects. P450 inhibitor, stop statins when used. Azithromycin can cause hearing loss and tinnitus
    Quinolones (-floxacin)Lower seizure threshold. Tendon damage. Prolongs QT. Avoid in pregnant/breastfeeding/G6PD
    TB - RifampicinPotent liver enzyme inducer. Hepatitis, Orange secretions. Prevents transcription of DNA to mRNA.
    TB - IsoniazidPeripheral neuropathy (add pyridoxine - vit B6). Hepatitis. Agranulocytosis. Inhibit mycolic acid synthesis.
    TB - PyrazinamideHyperuricaemia, gout. Hepatitis. Turns into pyrazinoic acid which inhibits FAS (fatty acid synthas)
    TB - EthambutolOptic neuritis - check visual acuity before and during treatment. Inhibit arabinan synthesis.
